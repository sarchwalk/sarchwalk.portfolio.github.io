CPSC 581 - Human Computer Interaction II, Project 2

Create a physical prototype to solve an everyday problem. The prototype must have at least one input and one output device, and a narrative storyboard sketch must describe the interactions with the final design.

Refined Sketches: Iterating on Punishing the Participant

After looking through our initial ideas, we agreed that we enjoyed the ones that involved a level of punishment. We thought that developing something that produces a not-so-ideal outcome would be fun to develop, and might have real applications in our lives. For our refined sketches, we iterated on the situation, the punishment, and the embodiment of the contraption, as we weren't entirely sure what was the best direction to go in.


Speed Dating: This idea explored having the system detect whether the user was facing an enemy or a crush. The spray would go off on an enemy, and perfume would spray on the user if it was a crush

Embodiment - Embedded in Computer Screen: This idea explored housing a spraying mechanism within a computer screen. The ideal application would be situations where the user is using their computer in ways that are not ideal.

Punishment - Eye Poison or Aphrodisiacs: These punishments were made for an earlier idea involving either punishing someone that approaches you by spraying them, or by spraying an aphrodisiac on yourself to encourage them to like you more. These ideas were not pursued because we went with a differed situation that did not require these sprays.

Embodiment - Police Interrogation: These sketches are based around an early idea we had involving a police interrogation. Erika sketched out different embodiment ideas to hide the spray within a body cam, and played around with different liquids that could be sprayed.

Embodiment - Tomb: This storyboard shows an earlier idea we had involving a tomb raider. The idea is that the spray would be used to punish the tomb raider and scare them away.

Punishment - Elastic Bands and Screeching: This idea was to have elastic bands shot at the user, all while a horrible screeching noise played. We decided not to go with the elastic bands because they did not have a clear way for implementation, but the screeching noise was something that we wanted to implement.

Situation - Playing Games When You Should Be Working: This sketch outlines an idea where the user should be working or studying, but instead plays games on their computer. This idea was considered for the final implementation, but we weren't as excited by it as we were the other ideas.

Embodiment - Treasure: These six sketches were different ideas for housing proposed by Erika. Most of these, excluding the golden idol and the treasure chest, were abandoned because there was no clear way to install our spray mechanism. We decided to go with the golden idol, or any sort of valuable looking statue, because we figured out a clear way to hide our spray mechanism within it that didn't make it obvious that it was booby-trapped.



Stone Warden

After iterating through different situations, punishments, and embodiments, we decided to make a booby-trapped statue that solves the thieving problem we face everyday. We call this the Stone Warden.


Narrative Storyboard

The Stone Warden is a statue that punishes thieves who try to steal it by spraying them with poison. The thief will then be so scared that they will leave in a hurry, ensuring that valuable items are left where they belong.

Our Ideal Client


We see individuals who want to protect their prized possessions wanting to use the Stone Warden. Ideally, they would place the statue on display amongst their other valuable items. The Stone Warden should be placed so that it is the easiest valuable to access, so that thieves try to take it first.



Embodiment


The Stone Warden is a stone statue with bright red flashing eyes with a hidden sensor and a mouth that sprays liquids. We designed the statue so that the input and output was hidden from the outside, ensuring that thieves aren't aware that the statue is booby-trapped.

One of the pieces of feedback we got from our classmates was to use proximity to trigger the spray. This proved useful as the ultrasonic sensor, which detects proximity, was easy to disguise as eyes in the statue. Red LED's were also used in the eyes to flash in anger while the spray went off.

To hide the water bladder, power supply, and the Arduino, the statue was put on a pedestal which contained the items. This also ensured that the electronics did not get wet from the spray if it were triggered.







The Hardware

The two LED's and the ultrasonic sensor are controlled and powered directly from the Arduino. The motor, which is a car windshield washer fluid spray motor, is powered by an external 12V power source and controlled by the Arduino through the use of a relay.
