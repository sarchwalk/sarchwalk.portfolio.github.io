The Gwyneth Paltrow Button

CPSC 581 - Human Computer Interaction II, Project 0

Sketch, design, implement and document via a web portfolio a software application where the central interaction is with a button, such that the interaction represents some aspect of your client's personality or character.

Client: Gwyneth Paltrow

Gwyneth Paltrow is an Academy Award winning actress known for her roles in movies such as Iron Man and Se7en. However, over the past few years public perception of her shifted in a new direction. Through her blog and the foundation of her lifestyle brand, goop, Gwyneth has promoted treatments that have greatly concerned medical professionals and the general public. Her recommendations have included vaginal steaming, bee sting facials, and coffee enemas. We chose Gwyneth as our client because we found her unique personality to be entertaining and oddly endearing.

Design Sketches

These sketches are of the characters I explored using the 10+10 method.

Gwyneth Paltrow Button: hover to see that Gwyneth has an idea, click to see a randomly selected animation of either v-steaming, pulling out and apple, a brain waving as two rings separate (conscious uncoupling), or bee sting facial.

Sailor Moon Button: click to initiate transformation sequence.

Britney Spears: left click to age up Britney, right click to age down.

Spongebob Button: click repeatedly to get Spongebob to blow a large bubble. The bubble envelopes him and becomes the button. Another idea was to flip a crabby patty on click.

Queen Elizabeth II: drag an item towards The Queen. The Queen approves corgis and matching jackets and hats. The Queen rejects Princess Diana.

Putin Button: click to answer phone call from The United States. Click again to hang up triggering the American flag to turn into the Russian flag.

Miley Cyrus Button: click and drag the wrecking ball to demolish Hannah Montana.

Oprah Button: click to see Oprah throwing a gift at you. Gets successively more expensive as click frequency climbs.

Slenderman Button: as Slenderman, the user points their cursor towards their victim. On click the victim gets eaten.

Kim Kardashian Button: click and drag Kim's earing into the ocean. Kim begins to cry.

After gaining feedback from our peers, we decided to go ahead with Gwyneth as our client. We felt that we could explore her randomness further and surprise users with her antics.

Refined Sketches

These sketches are the combined 10 from myself and my teammates.

Wheel lands on science: I explored having Gwyneth react negatively to the wheel landing on a science option. This action would be a play on Gwyneth's desire to do things as long as they are not supported by science.

Wheel lands on science: Gwyneth shakes her head no to say that she will not participate in the science option.

Wheel lands on science: Gwyneth reaches out and grabs the edge of the wheel. She then shifts the wheel so that it is no longer on the science option.

Wheel lands on science: Gwyneth covers the science image with her company logo, and begins shooting an episode of her show.

Wheel lands on science: Gwyneth screams so loud that the wheel shakes, causing it to shift.

Coffee Enema: I played around with the idea of pouring coffee on a peach to avoid being too graphic. Ultimately this idea was scrapped as we ran out of time.

Coffee Enema: my groupmate, Valerie, proposed the idea of having a cam for inflicted areas.

Other Fun Interactions: Valerie suggested that we make Gwyneth's head spin instead of the wheel. We considered implementing this when the user clicked Gwyneth instead of the wheel.

Other Fun Interactions: originally we thought that Gwyneth could stand beside the wheel. This could allow for more interaction between Gwyneth and the wheel.

Other Fun Interactions: Erika suggested that we depict Gwyneth as being sick, so that the wheel could choose her treatment. This was ultimately abandoned as we didn't want to give reasoning for Gwyneth's decisions.
